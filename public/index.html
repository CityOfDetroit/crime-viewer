Welcome to "TBD"!

The City of Detroit Police Department (DPD) and Department of Innovation and Technology (DoIT) are building a tool for residents to visualize crime data. By putting information in the hands of residents, we hope to facilitate a conversation about what is happening on a neighborhood level and the city at large. That way, we can develop solutions together towards a safer community.

How often is the data updated?

The open dataset which contains the incident data used by this tool is updated on a weekly basis and reflects incidents which have been contributed to the Michigan Incident Crime Reporting tool (the date of submission is reflected in the "IBR Date" field on Socrata).

How is the data anonymized?

The addresses and datapoints which appear in this tool are anonymized; instead of a specific street address, data points get assigned to a specific block. We do this by randomly selecting a new point that is 1) within a certain distance of the original crime and 2) a certain distance of the associated street segment. It does not accurately represent where the incident happened.

Where does the data come from?

The incidents that are used by the tool come from Detroit's open data portal. Find it at DPD: All Crime Incidents, December 6, 2016 - Present.

Disclaimer:
In order to protect the privacy of crime victims, both the addresses and geographic coordinates (longitude and latitude) are masked. See ABOUT to learn more.
